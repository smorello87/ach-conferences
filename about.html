ACH Conferences Archive

About This Project

What Is This?

This is an interactive data archive of presentations delivered at conferences organized or co-organized by the Association for Computers and the Humanities (ACH) from 1973 to 2024. It brings together over 2,700 records spanning five decades of digital humanities scholarship into a single searchable, filterable dashboard.

The archive makes it possible to trace the evolution of the field across time—from early work in concordance building, computational linguistics, and literary stylistics to contemporary research in digital pedagogy, cultural analytics, and critical DH.

Conference History

The ACH conference series has gone through several phases:

  • 1973–1987: ACH solo conferences at U.S. universities (also known as ICCH, the International Conference on Computers and the Humanities).
  • 1989–2006: Joint annual conferences with ALLC (Association for Literary and Linguistic Computing), rotating between North America and Europe.
  • 2019–2025: ACH renewed its own conference, held in Pittsburgh (2019), virtually (2022, 2025), Houston (2023), and Fairfax (2024).

The 2021 conference was held virtually in partnership with the University of Houston, but its program has not yet been indexed in any publicly accessible database.

Data Sources

Conference records from 1973 through 2022 are drawn from the Index of Digital Humanities Conferences, a relational database maintained by the University of Virginia Library. The full dataset is available as a relational dump or a simple CSV.

Records for ACH 2023 (Houston) and ACH 2024 (Fairfax) were parsed from ConfTool conference management exports. ACH 2025 (Virtual) program data was scraped from the conference website. Note that institutional affiliations are not available for 2025 presentations as they were not listed in the online program.

Where original keyword metadata was missing, topical keywords were generated using OpenAI GPT-5.2 based on presentation titles and, when available, author-supplied keywords. Generated keywords are cached to ensure consistency across builds.

What's in Each Record

Each presentation record includes:

  • Year and conference name
  • Presentation title and panel/session title (where applicable)
  • Presentation type (paper, poster, panel, workshop, etc.)
  • Affiliated institutions and countries
  • Topical keywords

Author names are deliberately excluded from the public dataset.

How It's Built

The data pipeline is written in Python. It joins 22 relational CSV files from the UVA database, filters to the ACH conference series, generates keywords for uncached works, and merges in the ConfTool records. The output is a single JSON file that powers the dashboard.

The dashboard itself is a single-file HTML application using D3.js v7 for all charts and TopoJSON for the geographic visualization. It is deployed via GitHub Pages.

Source code is available on GitHub.
